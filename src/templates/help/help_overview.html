Overview

What this App does

Welcome to the Film and Class Tracker Guide!

This app helps you keep track of your assignments and film work like shots and assets.

You can:

  • See what's due and who is working on what
  • Create and view videos of markups for revisions
  • Check and approve your grades
  • Update progress by submitting through your art software (like Maya or Harmony)

Tracker is split into two sections: CLASSES and FILMS.

The application is the "one stop shop" for anything assignment and shot/asset related: submissions, grades, retakes, and approvals.

Roles and What They Can Do

A user can be a student or instructor/admin.

A STUDENT can:

  • Submit assignments through animation software
  • Submit film shots and assets through animation software
  • View deadlines and status updates on their dashboard
  • View video markups of their work
  • Revise and resubmit assignments

An INSTRUCTOR can:

  • Add/Edit/Delete a Class
  • Add/Remove a Student from a Class
  • Add/Edit/Delete an Assignment
  • Update and Change Grades
  • Add/Edit/Delete a Film
  • View/Add/Edit/Delete a Scene
  • View/Add/Edit/Delete a Shot
  • Bulk Edit Shots
  • View/Add/Edit/Delete an Asset
  • Use the MARKUP system to draw on videos or images
  • Can create a config editor for Classes/Assignmnets or Films

An ADMIN can:

  • Add/Edit/Delete users and assign roles.
  • Configure Workflows
  • Manage archives
  • Oversee system-wide settings and tools

How Assignments and Shots Flow

The strength of the Shot Tracking app is allowing users to simply create and submit assignments and film shots/assets without worrying about naming convention or where to save or submit their files. This frees up the user to focus on the art for each assignment and shot.

🧾 Classes and Assignments

  1. Instructors add a class in the system and add students to that class.
  2. Instructors add assignments with start and due dates.
  3. Students submit work either through Maya, Harmony, or Storyboard Pro.
  4. Submissions are reviewed by the instructor using the markup tool.
  5. Status is updated to show progress (Submitted → Graded or Retake).
  6. Students can review a video of a markup and see grades and resubmit if needed.

🎬 Films, Scenes, and Shots

  1. A film project is created, broken into scenes and shots and assets.
  2. UPMs and admins can update schedules for pre-production and production
  3. UPMs and coordinators assign shots to students (e.g. Layout, Animation, FX).
  4. Students complete their assigned shots using the GAA shelf tools, or tools in Harmony, or Storyboard Pro,
  5. Submitted shots go into the review queue and are marked up or approved.
  6. Shots update their status as they move through the pipeline (WIP → Final).
  7. All assets and progress are tracked within the film’s dashboard, organized by step.
